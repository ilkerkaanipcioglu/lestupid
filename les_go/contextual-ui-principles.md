# LesTupid Go Contextual UI Principles

LesTupid UI should show the nature of the thing it represents. The interface is
not neutral decoration; it is a map of tempo, density, value, risk and place.

## Core Rule

- Fast things feel fast.
- Flowing things flow.
- Static things stand still.
- Beautiful/good things feel clear, generous and attractive.
- Bad/risky things feel visibly risky, not cute or hidden.
- Crowded places feel dense and active.
- Calm places feel quiet and breathable.
- Every app, place, product, person and event should show its own character.

## Layout Semantics

- Places are doors/homes/pages. They use stable shapes, fixed positions and
  calm motion.
- Feeds are streams. They use tempo, time labels and compact action surfaces.
- Live items are tighter, more urgent and lightly animated.
- Short-window items are compact and bright.
- Today/ongoing items are readable and useful, without panic.
- Stable items are larger, quieter and more grounded.
- Dense contexts show fewer cards by default but stronger priority.
- Calm contexts show more space, softer color and fewer competing actions.

## Product Mood

- `les_wait`: practical, time-aware, precise.
- `les_poke`: playful, quest-like, exploratory.
- `les_match`: consent-first, warm but careful.
- `lescommerce`: useful, priced, terms-visible.
- `les_contacts`: private, memory-like, calm.
- `les_harmonica`: protected, secure, restrained.
- `les_ai`: helpful, clearly labeled as AI.
- `les_certification`: sober, trustworthy, evidence-led.
- `les_itemotel`: custody, care, condition and recall clarity.

## Risk And Honesty

Risky, adult, medical, legal, financial or safety-related contexts must not be
made seductive. They should use plain labels, visible consent state, report
controls and clear next actions.

The UI can be beautiful without lying. Beauty means clarity, rhythm and fit.

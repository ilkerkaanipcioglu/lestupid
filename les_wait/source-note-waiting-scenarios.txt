Les Wait scenario notes

1. Valet / restaurant parking

A driver leaves a car with a valet. The valet records the plate, pickup time,
and parking note. The driver can see where the car is and request the car when
ready. The flow can also work through WhatsApp or Telegram.

2. Temporary blocked parking

A driver blocks another car briefly while buying medicine or handling an urgent
errand. The driver leaves a time-limited note. A blocked driver can scan the
plate, see the message, and contact the owner only through allowed channels.

3. Mall pizza / food court order

A customer orders pizza, receives a ticket, and scans the ticket number to see
the status. When the order is ready, the customer receives a notification. If
table delivery is available, the app can carry the table number.

4. Restaurant table order

A customer orders from the table by entering or scanning a table code. The
customer can track the food state and add items.

5. Waiter group order

A waiter records multiple people's orders at one table. Voice capture and AI can
help summarize who ordered what. Customers can review and correct their own
items.

6. Tattoo removal studio

A customer buys a package with multiple sessions, confirms appointment times,
and can also join a spontaneous queue. The queue entry links back to the package
session.

7. Barber

A customer books an appointment or scans a code to join a walk-in queue. The app
shows estimated wait time and notifies the customer when it is time to return.
If the barber steps out, the return time can be visible.

8. Taxi stand

A customer checks whether a taxi is available and sees the estimated arrival or
turn time.

Core principle: each scenario should work through app, web, QR, WhatsApp,
Telegram, SMS, staff entry, or manual/offline fallback.

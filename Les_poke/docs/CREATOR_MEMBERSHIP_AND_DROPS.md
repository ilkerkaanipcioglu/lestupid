# Les Poke Creator Membership And Drops

Les Poke can support creator membership, exclusive local drops and paid
quest/content access. It is not a sexual service marketplace.

## Product Shape

Creators can publish:

- public quest trails;
- member-only quest drops;
- concert, club, cafe, campus or city stories;
- paid behind-the-scenes posts;
- local creator challenges;
- sponsored creator promotion quests;
- live walk, reels/story, photo set and product demo challenges;
- fan club check-ins;
- collectible badges, points or proof events;
- adult creator content only where legal, 18+, consent-first and explicitly
  labeled.

## Static vs Flow

Static pages:

- creator profile;
- fan club;
- quest collection;
- city/place trail;
- membership tier;
- content library.

Flow events:

- new drop;
- quest unlock;
- paid access prompt;
- creator check-in;
- fan reaction;
- collab invite;
- creator promotion brief accepted;
- live content proof submitted;
- badge or point event;
- report/review event.

## Rules

- Public city quests remain usable without paid membership.
- Paid drops must be labeled before unlock.
- Sponsored creator promotion quests must be labeled before participation.
- Paid brief, payout, commission and affiliate terms stay in Les Commerce.
- Les Poke owns the challenge shape, proof boundary, point reward and location
  privacy.
- Private location trails are not sold.
- No hidden ranking boost for paying creators.
- Adult content requires age gate and legal review.
- Minors and school contexts cannot receive adult creator prompts.
- Money, gifts, access or travel must not be framed as payment for sexual
  services.
- Les Match people discovery still requires Les Match activation and both-side
  consent.

## First Use Cases

- A musician at a concert publishes a member-only recap quest.
- A cafe creator publishes a coffee route and private Q&A drop.
- A travel creator publishes a paid city trail.
- A campus creator publishes a study route for supporters.
- A club creator publishes an 18+ event recap where legal and age-gated.
- A cafe asks local creators for a short reels/story route, surfaced by Les Go
  and completed as a Les Poke quest.
- A DIY product page asks a creator to film material sellers, makers and
  ready-made items; checkout and campaign terms stay in Les Commerce.
